When choosing a protein bar, whether it be for pre/post workout, a meal replacement, or just a snack, it's difficult to compare the nutritional make up of the bars available all at once. No one has time to individually click through all the latest and greatest brands to find what will fit their needs. So I present you with a tool that takes the two flavors from each line available from 20+ popular protein bar brands.

There are a few factors to take into considerdation that waill be varient depending on your person goals. How much protein do you need? If you are lifting, or doing high-intensity workouts, it is suggested that your total grams of protein equal your lean body mass multiplied by 0.9-1.2. This will give your muscles the materials needed to rebuild after your workout. Otherwise, the typical suggestion is your total grams of protein equal your lean body mass multiplied by 0.6-0.9.

Other things to consider include the amount of sugar the bar contains and how much fat. Studies are surfacing that low carb-high fat diets are more beneficial than initially thought. Perhaps, though, you are the kind of person that values a bar with less fat. The graph aims to help you find what you are looking for independent of overall dietary suggestions.
